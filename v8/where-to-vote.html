Early Voting Tips

  • Early voting typically has shorter wait times than Election Day.
  • Weekday mornings and early afternoons usually have the shortest lines.
  • Bring a valid photo ID and your voter registration card if you have it.
  • Check wait times online before heading to a location to avoid long lines.

Vote by Mail Information

In Texas, you may be eligible to vote by mail if you are:

  • 65 years of age or older
  • Sick or disabled
  • Expected to give birth within three weeks before or after Election Day
  • Out of the county during early voting and on Election Day
  • Confined in jail but otherwise eligible to vote

How to Apply for a Mail-In Ballot

  1. 1

    Download the Application

    Download and print the Application for Ballot by Mail (ABBM) from the Texas Secretary of State website.

    Download Application
  2. 2

    Complete the Application

    Fill out all required fields, including your name, address, and the reason you're eligible to vote by mail. Be sure to sign and date the application.

  3. 3

    Submit Your Application

    Mail your completed application to your county's Early Voting Clerk. For Harris County, mail to:

    Harris County Elections Administrator

    P.O. Box 1148

    Houston, TX 77251-1148

  4. 4

    Receive and Return Your Ballot

    Once your application is approved, you'll receive your ballot by mail. Complete it and return it by mail or in person to your county's Early Voting Clerk before the deadline.

What to Bring to the Polls

Acceptable Forms of ID

In Texas, you must present one of these acceptable forms of photo ID:

  • Texas Driver License issued by the Texas Department of Public Safety (DPS)
  • Texas Election Identification Certificate issued by DPS
  • Texas Personal Identification Card issued by DPS
  • Texas Handgun License issued by DPS
  • United States Military Identification Card containing the person's photograph
  • United States Citizenship Certificate containing the person's photograph
  • United States Passport (book or card)

ID may be expired up to 4 years. For voters aged 70 or older, the ID may be expired for any length of time.

If You Don't Have an Acceptable ID

If you don't have an acceptable photo ID, you can still vote by signing a declaration and providing a supporting form of ID, such as:

  • Voter registration certificate
  • Certified birth certificate
  • Current utility bill
  • Bank statement
  • Government check
  • Paycheck
  • Other government document with your name and address

You'll need to sign a Reasonable Impediment Declaration stating why you couldn't obtain an acceptable photo ID. Valid reasons include work schedule, lack of transportation, disability, family responsibilities, lost or stolen ID, or pending application for ID.

  • Voter registration card
  • Sample ballot or notes

  • Water bottle
